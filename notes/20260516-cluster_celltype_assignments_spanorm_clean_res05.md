# Cluster cell-type assignments — SpaNorm clean (clusters 15 + 20 removed), sample.p = 0.25, Louvain 0.5

I assigned each of the 25 Louvain-0.5 clusters from the rebuilt `20260516_SpaNorm_samplep025_clean.qmd` using the top-10 FindAllMarkers per cluster (ranked by `avg_log2FC`; source: `results/20260516-find_all_markers_spanorm_p025_clean_res05.csv`). The panel is the 480-gene Xenium mBrain panel, so the cell-type vocabulary is constrained to what those probes resolve. I ordered the rows by cell-type similarity, not by cluster number, so related populations sit next to each other. The "Flagged genes" column lists top-10 markers that do not fit the called identity and that I would scrutinize as possible doublet or segmentation contamination before downstream DE.

**Scope note.** This build subset the parent SpaNorm object (`data/20260512_microglia_switch_spanorm_p025.qs2`) on `seurat_clusters %in% c("15", "20")` and dropped both before re-fitting SpaNorm per sample. The earlier 20260516 build had passed `c("20", "25")` and silently no-oped on the non-existent level "25", leaving cluster-15 cells in. Dropping both clusters at source eliminates the parent's bottom-centre slide imbalance and recovers the discrete cytotoxic-T-cell cluster (24 here) that the earlier buggy clean re-fit had lost.

## Summary by lineage

I split the 25 clean-0.5 clusters into:

* **Oligodendrocyte lineage (3 clusters):** OPCs (12), mature-OL high-FC core (0), mature-OL lower-FC Slide1-restricted twin (9).
* **Astrocytes (2 clusters):** gray-matter Aqp4+ (4) and Agt+ subtype (8).
* **Microglia (1 cluster):** cluster 2, still mixing homeostatic and DAM at top-10 strength; both DAM (Cst7 / Cd14 / Trem2) and homeostatic (Cx3cr1 / P2ry12 / Tmem119 / Siglech) markers in top-10. The homeostatic-vs-DAM split sits at 0.7 (clusters 11 / 5).
* **T cells (1 cluster, RECOVERED):** cluster 24 — the most consequential gain over the earlier buggy clean re-fit, which had lost this island.
* **Ependymal + choroid plexus (2 clusters):** 17 and 18; CP carries MHC-II (H2-Eb1 / H2-Ab1).
* **Vascular (3 clusters):** endothelial (5), VLMC + perivascular macrophages (7), pericytes (15). Cleaner endothelial top-10 than the parent.
* **Excitatory neurons (6 clusters):** deep-layer Fezf2+ (1), Calb2+/Nts+/Tac1+ mixed (3), Cux2+/Lamp5+ upper-layer with IEG load (6), Vglut2+/Rorb+ thalamic relay (10), Tshz2+ deep / hippocampal (19), Wif1+/Syt6+ (21).
* **GABAergic interneurons (4 clusters):** Sst+/Lhx6+ MGE (11), striatal Tac1+/Adcy5+/Pax6+ (14), Vip+/Crh+ CGE (20), Pvalb+/Trh+ (22).
* **Small / atypical (2 clusters):** Pou3f1+ stressed neurons (13), Lypd1+/Slc17a7+ small neuron-like (16).
* **Mixed / drop before DE (1 cluster):** low-FC Slc32a1+/Mbp+ multi-lineage doublets (23, Slide2-private).

The cluster-9 / cluster-0 oligodendrocyte split is the load-bearing slide effect, but I do not yet know whether it is biological (regional / maturation OL pools) or a transcript-yield normalization residual. Slide2 carries +25 % transcripts / cell vs Slide1 (CLAUDE.md QC gotcha); proportional OL fractions are comparable across the two slides (18.3 % vs 16.5 %), so the split is a redistribution of OLs across two FC tiers, not a difference in the OL fraction itself. Distinguishing the two hypotheses requires (i) a spatial overlay of cluster 9 cells on the slide images, (ii) per-cluster nCount_Xenium / nFeature_Xenium split by slide, and (iii) a re-fit with `df.tps = 12` or per-slide TPS basis. Cluster 23 is a Slide2-private doublet cluster and should be dropped. The other entries (clusters 2, 13, 19, 24) populate both slides at ≥ 94 cells and reflect biological / sample-level variation rather than slide-wide effects.

## Verdict and recommended cleanup before downstream DE

Removing parent clusters 15 + 20 (reactive doublets and Calb1+/Tdo2+ regional neurons) was the right call: the clean build resolves the cytotoxic-T-cell cluster (24), removes the parent-build bottom-centre slide imbalance, and produces a 25-cluster decomposition with one residual Slide2-private doublet cluster (23) that should be dropped at the cell-selection step. The Slide1-restricted mature-OL lower-FC twin (cluster 9) is reproducible across builds and Slide1-private; I keep it pending the spatial overlay + per-cluster nCount check that would distinguish a real regional / maturation OL pool from a transcript-yield normalization residual.

I would (1) re-run `FindClusters(resolution = 0.7)` (already cached in `xen$clusters_res07`) to recover the homeostatic-vs-DAM microglia split before the E4-vs-E2 contrast, (2) drop cluster 23 cells before pseudobulk DE, (3) sub-cluster microglia (2) through `20260517_microglia_subcluster.qmd` for finer microglia states, (4) `AddModuleScore()`-filter cluster 13 (Pou3f1+/Lpl+ stressed) and cluster 14 (Cd36+/Ido1+ striatal) for contamination before DE, (5) confirm cluster 16 (Lypd1+/Slc17a7+) is excitatory rather than inhibitory by checking Gad1 enrichment, and (6) spatially inspect cluster 24 (T cells) relative to amyloid plaques.
